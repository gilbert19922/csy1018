The first three jobs I compared are all looking for similar experience working in website development. They require good HTML, CSS and JavaScript skills and are looking for alternative skills in areas such as PHP. The previous experience is in either website development or website design none are looking for you to have both as they are two different jobs. All the jobs are looking for you to have good abilities at working with a team, this is due to the nature of having to work with people who will design the website, others who will elicit what the external company is actually looking for. Then the information will be passed to the website developer to build the website.

They all have similar salaries ranging from £20,000 to £35,000 but are aimed at people with at least 5 years industry experience. An experienced web developer can expect a salary of around £22,000 to £45,000 assuming they have skills in C# programming or alternative qualifications.

The final job I have posted is for an IT support/network engineer, I have added this job as it is an area that I would like to work in and has very different requirements compared to the web development jobs. A good reference for this is the required experience, a network engineer will not be required to have little/no experience working with website development tools like HTML and CSS but will be required to have experience with Microsoft Office and hardware components like switches and routers.

The salary for this job is lower than the one for the website development salary at £18,000 to £22,000 a part of this is the fact they are only looking for 3 years industry experience. A network engineer with 5 years industry experience would be looking at a salary of around £25,000 to £50,000 depending on skills acquired throughout their career, a person with a cisco accredited qualification will be on the higher end of the pay scale.
